\chapter{Conclusion}

This paper has provided an overview of prequalification tests for two popular ancillary services: \textit{FCR} and \textit{aFRR}. The prequalification test provides permission to the interested participants to bid in the market and serve ancillary services and it is a crucial step for the market participants to demonstrate the strength of their system to take actions in the power system. The electricity market always follows a standard structure for all market actors but an inevitable difference emerges in the balancing market over time. Thus, some changes are expected in the prequalification test for FCR and aFRR markets all time.
